Milestones in Space

2013
India's Pride
Mangalyaan — Mars Orbiter Mission

India became the first Asian country to reach Mars orbit, completing the mission on its very first attempt — a feat no other space agency had achieved. SkyLakes draws inspiration from this audacious milestone.

2023
Lunar Triumph
Chandrayaan-3 — South Pole Landing

India's Chandrayaan-3 made history as the first spacecraft to land near the lunar south pole — a triumph of Indian engineering that redefined what's possible at a fraction of global mission costs.

2017
Record-Breaking
104 Satellites — One Rocket

ISRO shattered the world record by launching 104 satellites on a single PSLV rocket, proving India's unmatched capability in cost-effective orbital delivery — the very foundation SkyLakes is built upon.

2026
The Future
SkyLakes — First Reusable Launch

SkyLakes AeroSpace prepares for its inaugural reusable rocket launch — bringing SpaceX-level capabilities to India's commercial space sector, making orbital access more affordable than ever before.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked
Questions

What makes SkyLakes rockets reusable?
+
Our rockets feature autonomous propulsive landing systems, reinforced thermal protection, and modular components designed for rapid refurbishment. Each vehicle undergoes a comprehensive health check between flights, enabling up to 10 safe re-flights with turnaround times as short as 72 hours.
How does SkyLakes reduce launch costs?
+
By recovering and reusing the most expensive parts of the rocket — the first stage and engine — we eliminate up to 70% of per-launch hardware costs. Combined with our India-based supply chain and indigenous manufacturing, we can offer launch prices far below the global average.
What payload capacity does SkyLakes offer?
+
Our initial vehicle class targets LEO payloads of 1,500 kg with a rideshare-friendly configuration. We are developing a medium-lift variant targeting 6,000 kg to GTO, designed to serve the growing commercial geostationary satellite market.
Where is SkyLakes based and where will it launch from?
+
SkyLakes AeroSpace is headquartered in Bengaluru, India's aerospace hub. Our primary launch site will leverage existing spaceport infrastructure on India's east coast, with plans for a dedicated private launch facility by 2028.
How can I partner with or invest in SkyLakes?
+
We welcome partnerships with satellite operators, government agencies, academic institutions, and strategic investors who share our vision. Reach out via our contact form below — our business development team typically responds within 48 hours.
